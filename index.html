About

I am a Health AI researcher working on learning systems for clinical imaging and live cell microscopy, with a focus on reliable evaluation, efficient training, and deployment-ready code. My recent work includes methods for prostate Gleason grading, metastasis detection in lymph node whole-slide images, and robust nuclei segmentation and tracking in microscopy data.

I am currently based in Toronto, working with the QUANTIMB Lab and the Lassonde School of Engineering at York University. I am broadly interested in machine learning for digital pathology, computational imaging, and high-throughput cellular analytics.

Research Interests

I work at the intersection of machine learning, medical imaging, and digital microscopy. Methodologically, I am interested in:

  • Weak supervision & multiple-instance learning for whole-slide images
  • Representation learning under distribution shift (stain & scanner variation)
  • Segmentation and instance-level tracking in time-lapse microscopy
  • Efficient, deployment-ready models for large images and resource-constrained hardware

Application domains include prostate Gleason grading, breast cancer lymph node metastasis detection, and quantitative analysis of smooth muscle cell behavior in live cell microscopy.
